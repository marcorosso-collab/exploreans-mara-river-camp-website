The Maasai have lived alongside the wildlife of the Mara for generations. A visit to a nearby village offers a glimpse into daily life — the construction of homes, the role of cattle, the beadwork and craftsmanship passed down through families.

The Maasai dance is not a performance staged for visitors. It is a living tradition — the leaping, the chanting, the deep rhythmic pulse of community. You are welcome to watch, and often invited to join.
